# Aqua-AI Distribution Checklist & Schedule

This checklist provides a step-by-step guide to executing the community launch for Aqua-AI. Following this schedule will help maximize visibility and engagement.

## Phase 0: Pre-Launch Readiness (Check these first!)
- [ ] **Repository Polish**: README is professional, includes "Star on GitHub" CTA, and has a high-quality social preview image.
- [ ] **Frontend Live**: The React application is deployed and the "Star on GitHub" banner/notification is active.
- [ ] **Visual Assets**: At least 3 high-quality screenshots (Dashboard, Map, AI Insights) and one 15-second GIF of the app in action are ready.
- [ ] **Link Check**: All links in the outreach templates point to the correct repository: `https://github.com/Kuldeep2822k/aqua-ai`.

---

## Phase 1: The Launch Sequence (First 48 Hours)

### Day 1: Developer & Technical Communities
*Focus on technical feedback and building initial momentum.*

1. **r/DevelopersIndia (Morning IST - 9:00 AM)**
   - Use the "Developer Showcase" template.
   - Attach the best screenshot of the full dashboard.
   - Respond to all comments within 1 hour.
2. **Dev.to & Hashnode (Afternoon IST - 2:00 PM)**
   - Publish the "How I built..." blog post.
   - Share the blog post links on LinkedIn/X.
3. **r/MachineLearning or r/DataScience (Evening IST / Morning EST - 6:30 PM IST)**
   - Use the "Technical Reddit" template.
   - Focus on the TensorFlow architecture and data source integration.

### Day 2: Impact & Regional Communities
*Focus on social impact and broader awareness.*

1. **r/India (Morning IST - 10:00 AM)**
   - Use the "Impact/Regional" template.
   - Lead with the "Made in India" and "Sustainability" angles.
2. **r/Sustainability (Afternoon IST / Morning EST - 3:00 PM IST)**
   - Focus on the environmental monitoring aspect.
3. **LinkedIn & X (X-post throughout the day)**
   - Use the punchy "Hook" template.
   - Tag relevant influencers in the climate-tech and Indian dev space.

---

## Phase 2: Engagement & Momentum Strategy

- **Respond to Feedback**: Check Reddit and Dev.to every 4-6 hours for the first 2 days. Answer technical questions thoroughly.
- **Thank Contributors**: If someone leaves a thoughtful comment or a star, thank them if the platform allows (e.g., replying to comments).
- **Update README**: If you get a recurring question, add the answer to a "FAQ" section in your README to show you are listening.
- **Social Proof**: Once you hit 10/25/50 stars, post a small "Thank You" update on X/LinkedIn to maintain interest.

---

## Phase 3: Tracking & Audit

- **GitHub Insights**: Monitor `Insights -> Traffic` to see which platforms are driving the most visitors.
- **Star History**: Use a tool like `star-history.com` to track the growth curve.
- **Conversion Rate**: Compare "Unique Visitors" vs "New Stars" to evaluate the effectiveness of your README and "Star" banner.
- **Final Audit**: After 1 week, record the total stars gained and the top-performing community.

---

**Next Steps:** Proceed to execute the launch according to this schedule. Good luck! 🚀
